Earthquake Guidelines

10 Tips

Drop, Cover, and Hold On: When you feel the shaking of an earthquake, follow the "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" technique. Drop to the ground to prevent being knocked over, take cover under a sturdy piece of furniture, and hold on until the shaking stops.

Stay Indoors: If you are inside a building during an earthquake, avoid running outside as falling debris can be dangerous. Instead, stay indoors, away from windows, glass, and heavy objects that could fall.

Stay Clear of Hazards: Keep away from windows, glass doors, and exterior walls to avoid injury from shattered glass. Stay clear of heavy furniture, hanging objects, and appliances that could topple over.

Find Safe Spots: Identify safe spots in every room, such as under sturdy tables or against interior walls. Avoid standing near doorways as they are not as safe as commonly thought.

Be Mindful of Elevators: If you are in a building, avoid using elevators during an earthquake as they may become stuck or malfunction.

Stay Calm: Keep calm during the earthquake and reassure others around you. Panic can lead to accidents and injuries.

Evacuate if Necessary: If you are in a coastal area and feel a strong earthquake, move immediately to higher ground as it could trigger a tsunami.

After the Shaking Stops: Check yourself and others for injuries. Be prepared for aftershocks and remain cautious as they may cause further damage.

Listen to Authorities: Follow the instructions given by local authorities and emergency services. Be aware of emergency evacuation routes and shelters in your area.

Prepare an Emergency Kit: Have an earthquake emergency kit ready with essential supplies like food, water, first aid items, flashlight, batteries, a battery-operated radio, and necessary medications.
