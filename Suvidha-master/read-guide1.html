Handbook For Floods

Surviving a flood requires careful preparation, quick decision-making, and the ability to adapt to rapidly changing conditions. Floods can be highly dangerous and life-threatening, so it's essential to take the necessary steps to protect yourself and your loved ones. Here's a comprehensive guide to survival in floods:

Before the Flood:
Stay informed: Monitor weather forecasts and warnings regularly. Pay attention to local news and updates from official sources to be aware of any potential flood threats in your area.
Create an emergency plan: Develop a flood emergency plan with your family. Determine evacuation routes, meeting points, and communication methods. Make sure everyone in the family knows the plan and practices it.
Prepare an emergency kit: Assemble a well-stocked emergency kit that includes essential supplies like non-perishable food, bottled water, first aid supplies, a flashlight, batteries, a battery-operated radio, necessary medications, personal documents, important phone numbers, and clothing.
Secure your property: Elevate electrical appliances and utilities above flood level if possible. Waterproof your basement, and install check valves in plumbing to prevent sewage backup.
Know your surroundings: Be aware of your community's flood risk, and identify higher ground or safe areas where you can evacuate if needed.

During the Flood:
Evacuation: If authorities issue a flood warning or an evacuation order, follow it immediately. Don't wait for the water levels to rise; leaving early is safer.
Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters: Just six inches of moving water can knock you off your feet, and only two feet of water can carry away most vehicles. Stay away from flooded areas, including bridges and roads.
Move to higher ground: If you are trapped in a building during a flood, move to the highest level possible. Avoid going to the attic, as you may become trapped there if the water rises too high.
Listen to emergency alerts: Stay tuned to local radio or official sources for updates and instructions. Follow any directions provided by emergency services.
Use electricity safely: If floodwaters are advancing in your area, turn off electricity at the main breaker to prevent electrical shocks. Never use electrical appliances or touch electrical equipment if you are wet or standing in water.
Avoid floodwater contact: Floodwater can be contaminated with sewage, chemicals, and other hazardous materials. Avoid direct contact with it as much as possible.

After the Flood:
Wait for the all-clear: Don't return to your home or affected area until authorities have declared it safe to do so.
Inspect your property: Before re-entering your home, check for structural damage and be cautious of potential hazards like loose wires or gas leaks. Wear protective gear like rubber boots and gloves.
Clean and disinfect: Thoroughly clean and disinfect your home, belongings, and personal items that came into contact with floodwater to prevent mold and health risks.
Contact your insurance company: File a claim with your insurance company as soon as possible and document the damage with photographs.
Seek assistance: If your home has sustained significant damage and you're unable to stay with friends or family, seek assistance from local authorities or relief organizations.
Remember, floods can be unpredictable, and safety should always be your top priority. Always follow the advice and warnings from local emergency services and officials. Being prepared and knowing what to do can make a significant difference in your ability to survive and recover from a flood.
